Table 3. Clinical Decision Matrix: Segmental vs Larger Resection in Elderly Hepatobiliary Cancer
=========================================================================================================
Patient Profile                         | Recommendation      | Rationale
----------------------------------------|---------------------|------------------------------------------
Age ≥70 + HCC + Stage I-II              | Segmental/Wedge     | Equivalent survival, less morbidity
Age ≥70 + ICC + Any Stage               | Segmental/Wedge     | HR 0.22 vs 0.26 favoring segmental
Age 65-69 + HCC + Stage III-IV          | Larger considered   | Marginally better HR in advanced dz
Age ≥75 + Any cancer + Frail            | Segmental/Wedge     | Benefit persists even in frail (HR 0.33)
Any age + Transplant candidate          | Transplant          | HR 0.15 — strongest protection
Age ≥80 + Any cancer                    | Segmental (if surg) | HR 0.26-0.31; age alone ≠ contraindication

Evidence level: Observational (SEER N=76,110 + TCGA + ICGC). All surgical comparisons
are adjusted for measured confounders but remain subject to unmeasured confounding (E-value ~1.3
for segmental vs larger comparison).
